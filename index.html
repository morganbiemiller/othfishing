The Captain

Meet Captain Ken Biemiller

  • US Coast Guard licensed Master Captain.
  • Grew up in South Miami, Florida: fishing Miami to the Florida Keys.
  • Moved to Rochester, NY in 1984 and returned to my passion of sport fishing in 1989 on Lake Ontario.
  • I have 25 years of fishing experience on Lake Ontario and would like to share it with you.

Boat & Gear

Boat

"Off The Hook II"

Trojan International 10 Meter Express 37' LOA
Manufactured by Bertram Yachts: "Feel the Ride, Live the Legend"
"The Great Lakes battle wagon"

A 37' LOA, 10-meter Trojan International Express: Sportfishing's ultimate platform. There is no better designed boat to experience what Lake Ontario has to offer. Step on Board Off The Hook II and you'll immediately notice its massive 13.3-beam. Not only does this make it comfortable, but also it gives the boat the ability to handle pretty much whatever the lake has to dish out plus the stability of smooth trolling all day long.

Fish

Coho Salmon

Species Facts

Science Name: Oncorhynchus kisutch

Other Names: Silver Salmon, Blueback

Ideal Temp: 53-58 degrees

World Record: 33lbs. 4oz., NY

Environment: Coastal, stream, lake

Techniques: Medium casting, medium fly

Range: Great Lakes and western North America

Chinook Salmon

Species Facts

Science Name: Oncorhynchus tshawytscha

Other Names: King Salmon, Spring Salmon, Tyee, Quinnant, Blackmouth

Ideal Temp: 52-58 degrees

World Record: 97lbs., 4oz,. Alaska

Environment: Stream and coastal

Techniques: Medium casting, medium fly

Lake Trout

Species Facts

Science Name: Salvelinus namaycush

Other Names: Laker, Mackinaw, Gray Trout, Togue

Ideal Temp: 50-57 degrees

World Record: 72lbs., NW Terr.

Environment: Lakes

Techniques: Medium tackle, bottom fishing, downrigger

Brown Trout

Species Facts

Science Name: Salmo trutta

Other Names: German Brown, Loch Leven Trout, Brownie, Sea Trout

Ideal Temp: 56-66 degrees

World Record: 40lbs., 4oz., Arkansas

Environment: Stream, lake

Techniques: Light tackle, light fly, casting

Rainbow/
Steelhead

Species Facts

Science Name: Oncorhynchus mykiss

Other Names: Rainbow Trout, Bow, Redsides, Red-Band Trout, Silver Trout, Steelhead, Kamloops

Ideal Temp: 55-70 degrees

World Record: 42lbs., 2oz., Alaska

Environment: Lake, stream, coastal

Techniques: Light tackle, light fly

What to Pack

Pack

  • NYS Fishing License
    • 16 years or older need a license. Please let Capt. know if you need assistance with the licenses.
  • Food & drinks
    • For our safety, cans or soft bottles only please.
  • Soft-sole boat shoes
    • No black soles.
  • Sun protection
  • Rain gear
  • Warm clothes
  • Camera
  • Cooler for your catch
  • Motion sickness medication if needed
  • Tip for the crew
  • No illegal drugs
